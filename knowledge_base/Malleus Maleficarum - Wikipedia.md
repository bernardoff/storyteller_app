#### According to Jenny Gibbons:

in the 1970s, when feminist and Neo-Pagan authors turned their attention to the witch trials, the

Malleus Maleficarum

Hammer of Witches

) was the only manual readily available in translation. Authors naively assumed that the book painted an accurate picture of how the Inquisition tried witches. Heinrich Kramer, the text's demented author, was held up as a typical inquisitor. His rather stunning sexual preoccupations were presented as the Church's "official" position on witchcraft. Actually the Inquisition immediately rejected the legal procedures Kramer recommended and censured the inquisitor himself just a few years after the Malleus was published. Secular courts, not inquisitorial ones, resorted to the

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-Gibbons,\_Jenny\_pp\_2\_-\_16-28

Before 1400 it was rare for anyone to be prosecuted for witchcraft, but the increasingly common prosecution of heresy and failure to fully defeat these heretics paved the way for later criminal prosecution of witchcraft.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTEPavlac200931-29

By the 15th century belief in witches was widely accepted in European society. Previously, those convicted of witchcraft typically suffered penalties no more harsh than public penances such as a day in the

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stocks

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-reform-22

but their prosecution became more brutal following the publication of the

Malleus Maleficarum

, as witchcraft became

clarificationneeded

widely accepted as a real and dangerous phenomenon.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTETrevor-Roper1969102%E2%80%93105-30

The most severe prosecutions took place between the years 1560 and 1630, largely ending in Europe around 1780.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTELevack201374-31

Particularly in the 16th and 17th centuries an intense debate on the nature of witches preoccupied

demonologists

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demonologist

across Europe and they published many printed sermons, books and tracts. The

Catholic Church

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic\_Church

played an important role in shaping of debate on demonology, but the discourse was not much affected by the Reformation.

Martin Luther

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martin\_Luther

was also convinced about the reality and evil of witches, and facilitated development of

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestant

demonology.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTELevack201375%E2%80%9376-32

Catholic and Protestant demonologies were similar in their basic beliefs about witches

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTELevack201379-33

and most writers agreed on the severity of the crime of witchcraft.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTELevack201377-34

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-36

It was accepted by both Catholic and Protestant legislatures

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTESummers2012viiiIntroduction\_to\_1948\_edition-35

and witch-hunting was undeniably sponsored by both Protestant and Catholic governments.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTEAnkarlooClark2002b10-37

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-39

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-70

The treatise is divided into three sections. The first section is aimed at clergy and tries to refute critics who deny the reality of witchcraft, thereby hindering its prosecution.

The second section describes the actual forms of witchcraft and its remedies. The third section is to assist judges confronting and combating witchcraft, and to aid the inquisitors by removing the burden from them. Each of the three sections has the prevailing themes of what is witchcraft and who is a witch.

Kramer and Sprenger develop a powerful sex-specific theory of witchcraft based on a hierarchical and dualistic view of the world. Everything exists in pairs of opposites: God and Satan, Mary and Eve, and men (or virgins) and women. Each positive principle in a pair is delineated by its negative pole. Perfection is defined not as the integration or preservation of opposites, but rather as the extermination of the negative element in a polar pair. Because women are the negative counterpart to men, they corrupt male perfection through witchcraft and must be destroyed.

https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malleus\_Maleficarum#cite\_note-FOOTNOTEBrauner200138-96
