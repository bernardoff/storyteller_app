During the Dark Medieval, some Telyavelic Tremere sympathized with Goratrix's policies of empowerment.

The Tremere

denounced Tremere himself, and devoted themselves to the principles of freedom espoused by the nascent

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/Sabbat

. Some rebelled against the rigid hierarchy of the Dark Ages Tremere, while others simply saw in Goratrix a more charismatic leader or a chance at personal advancement in a sect that denounced the manipulations of elders.

Among the Sabbat Tremere, dilettantes, reporters and professionals are among the most commonly Embraced individuals. Most

are deviants or visionaries that manage to maintain a relatively "normal" appearance. Unlike the other Sabbat clans, they favor knowledge and intellect over brute strength, as most spend their time focusing on mystical matters rather than the usual Sabbat hellraising. They also do not Embrace during times of war. Unlike the Camarilla Tremere, the Spellbinders favor raw intelligence and willingness to learn over ambition and drive, but most fledglings undergo a period of apprenticeship as in the main clan.

Organization \[

Most Tremere

were members of

House Goratrix

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/House\_Goratrix

, forming a private mystical brotherhood within the Sabbat that somewhat mirrored the original clan's Hermetic structure. At the top of the pyramid sat Goratrix, and beneath him was a Council of Six, in parody of the Council of Seven's fascination with numerology. Below that, there was a Jesuit-like rule of obedience to

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/Sire

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/Pack\_(VTM)

. Members of this pyramid were scattered among different Sabbat packs, but retained closer links with each other than with their packmates. Those outside House Goratrix were more heterodox and less likely to obey this structure, although they still encouraged respect towards the bloodline's leaders.

In Europe, the

maintained their own

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/Chantry\_(VTM)

outside of Sabbat temples, having had centuries to build them, even before the

Anarch Revolt

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/Anarch\_Revolt

. Many of these were built in the wilderness, designed to hide the rogues from the main clan in Vienna. Additionally, the prominence of both the

https://whitewolf.fandom.com/wiki/Lasombra

and the mainline clan meant that the European

competed with these clans rather than with the Tzimisce, as in America.

The North American

, having more recent foundations, tended to form labs by cordoning off areas within Sabbat temples. The exception was New York and Mexico City, where the Tremere

had their own chantries away from the Sabbat communal havens. This made the American

more integrated with the rest of the sect than their European counterparts. Individual members typically had their own private sanctuaries even if they used a Sabbat temple for their lab.

spent much time in study and arcane work, meaning they were almost always members of founded covens. They favored loyalty within the bloodline more than other clans in the sect. They also made their childer undergo an apprenticeship, like the main clan. Every seven years, the bloodline held a meeting called a synod, which all members were invited to. Members could climb the ranks through creating new rituals, manipulating the sect for the bloodline's own ends, or harming the mainline clan.
